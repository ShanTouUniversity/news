It is reported that the Party Committee of the STU Engineering College, Shantou Science Museum, and Waimalu No. 4 Primary School will co-host the "Surfing the Tech Waves, Sailing into the Intelligent Era" Science and Technology Exhibition on June 14 at Shantou Science Museum. The event is open to primary and secondary school students and residents of Shantou.

Organized by the Youth League Committee of the Engineering College, the exhibition will feature five interactive booths: "Gearbox: The Magical World of Gears," "Electronic Hourglass: The Digital Dance of Time," "Jiligu Vehicle: Ancient 'Odometer' Technology," "Line-Following Car: Smart Cars That 'Know the Way,'" and "Drones: Little Tech Sprites in the Sky." Students from Waimalu No. 4 Primary School will serve as science guides, using a "guide + demonstration + interactive experience" format to introduce attendees to mechanical principles, electronics, intelligent control, and aerospace technology.

A stamp-collection activity will also be held. Participants who complete experience tasks at each booth can collect stamps, and those who gather the required number can redeem gifts.
