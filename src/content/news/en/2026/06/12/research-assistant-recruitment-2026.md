It is reported that the Institute for New Quality Productivity is recruiting undergraduate research assistants to strengthen the institute's development, cultivate innovative research talents, and enhance students' scientific research capabilities.

The institute was initiated by Shantou University and CKGSB Business School, with guidance from the World Academy of Digital Science and the World Academy of Sciences and Engineers, and is hosted at the STU Business School. It focuses on the basic theory of new quality productivity, regional models, international cooperation, and open innovation.

Recruitment primarily targets first- and second-year undergraduate students, with a focus on Business School students. Students from other departments who have a strong interest in digital strategy and innovation entrepreneurship research are also welcome. Applicants need not have mastered complete research methods; the institute places greater emphasis on genuine research interest, willingness to commit, independent thinking ability, and growth potential.

Key requirements are as follows:

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Mandatory | Strong interest in further study, academic aspirations, and scientific research |
| Mandatory | Strong sense of responsibility and ability to commit time and effort long-term and consistently |
| Mandatory | Strong initiative, learning ability, and exploratory spirit; proficient in using AI tools while maintaining independent judgment |
| Mandatory | Basic data processing and Chinese/English writing skills |
| Preferred | Literature retrieval skills; familiarity with STATA/Python or other research data software |
| Preferred | Academic ranking within the top 20% of major |

Work includes participating in horizontal projects such as industrial digital transformation research, Chaoshan merchant case library construction, and overseas Chinese economic policy analysis, as well as vertical projects such as the National Natural Science Foundation of China. Tasks include field research, data collection, report writing, literature retrieval, data analysis, and drafting.

Research assistants will receive comprehensive research skill training, one-on-one mentoring by academic advisors, stable remuneration, academic publication credit, opportunities to attend domestic and international academic conferences, and for outstanding performers, academic advancement resources or internship recommendations.

The appointment is divided into two phases: Phase I (July 1 to August 31, 2026) and Phase II (September 1, 2026 to June 30, 2027), with renewal contingent on performance during Phase I.

Interested students should submit their application materials, including a personal introduction and representative work, to qguo@stu.edu.cn before 6:00 PM on June 25, with the subject line "Institute Application - Name - Major - Grade". Interviews will be conducted by June 18.
