It is reported that, ahead of the final examination period, the Gezhi College Learning Mutual Aid Center will launch professional course tutoring activities to help residents prepare effectively, while simultaneously recruiting tutors and students in need of academic support.

The sessions will be held from 20:00 to 21:00 on June 11 in Building E dormitory, with Criminal Law tutoring in Room E204 and Applied Statistics in Room E301. The activity adopts a "one-to-many" group mutual aid format, providing systematic guidance on study methods, mind map creation, answer logic, and calculation techniques.

Four tutors are being recruited from all Gezhi College students — two for Criminal Law and two for Applied Statistics — with a minimum grade requirement of 83 in the respective subject, along with a sense of responsibility and willingness to share. All students facing difficulties in these two courses are welcome to participate as learners, with no limit on numbers. Tutors will receive corresponding volunteer service hours, and outstanding participants and study groups may be awarded the title of "Gezhi Mutual Aid Pioneer."

Interested students may fill out the corresponding registration questionnaire; successful applicants will be added to a group chat for follow-up notices.
