It is reported that the Institute of Mathematics will host the 950th session of the "Towards Modern Mathematics" academic lecture series on June 25 at 9:30 at East Coast Campus D-209, featuring Dr. Shen Haishuang from Guangdong University of Education.

**Lecture Details**

- Topic: Parabolic-Hyperbolic Model of Tumor Growth with Angiogenesis
- Time: June 25, 9:30
- Venue: East Coast Campus, D-209
- Invited by: Dr. Lin Qingze

**Abstract**

This talk focuses on a parabolic–hyperbolic system for tumor growth, a coupled system of PDEs with Robin boundary conditions involving nutrient density, extracellular matrix, and matrix-degrading enzymes. By transforming the free boundary into a fixed boundary, the existence and uniqueness of a radially symmetric stationary solution is proved. Using the fixed point theorem, global-in-time existence and uniqueness of the radially symmetric solution is obtained.

**About the Speaker**

Shen Haishuang earned his PhD from the School of Mathematics at Sun Yat-sen University in 2023. He is currently a faculty member at Guangdong University of Education, specializing in stability analysis and qualitative analysis of tumor growth models. His research has been published in *J. Differential Equations* and *Nonlinear Anal. Real World Appl.*
