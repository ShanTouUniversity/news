Launched in June 2025, the Intelligent Systems Innovation Class at the College of Engineering uses interdisciplinary project-based training to cultivate engineering talent. The 2026 session is now open for applications.

## Program Highlights

- **Interdisciplinary training**: Integrates mechanical, electronic, civil, and biomedical engineering with a "mentor + industry expert" dual guidance model
- **Practice-oriented teaching**: Enterprise visits, real-world project development, and innovation competitions through university-industry collaboration
- **Certification & incentives**: Graduates receive an Innovation Class certificate and 2 elective credits. Outstanding members may receive funding for patent applications, publications, and provincial-level competition participation

## Selection Process

**Eligibility**: Engineering undergraduates (2024 and 2025 cohorts) with a GPA in the top 30% of their major and year.

**Process**: Application review → Interview (personal presentation + expert Q&A) → **45 students** selected based on comprehensive evaluation.

## Schedule

- **Application deadline**: **June 10**, 5:30 PM
- **Interview**: June 14 (venue to be announced)
- **How to apply**: Fill in the Tencent Docs registration form
