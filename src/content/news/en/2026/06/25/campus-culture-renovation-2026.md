The university has launched a campus culture renovation project starting June 25, upgrading the campus signage system. Existing signs suffer from aging materials and improper installation, posing aesthetic and safety concerns.

Work includes installing a spirit landmark, upgrading bulletin boards and guideposts, and adding ground-level cultural markers. Construction areas include the lawn in front of the Truth Bell, the lawn in front of the Administration Building, and existing signage locations. Work is expected to last through July 3, subject to weather delays.

Students and staff are advised to keep clear of construction areas.
