The STU Student Affairs Office has launched its **2026 Student Financial Aid Integrity Education Campaign**, requiring all colleges to organize activities focused on integrity, financial literacy, and fraud prevention. Colleges must submit their activity reports by **June 3**.

## Key Activities

The campaign encompasses four main components:

- **Surveys**: Gathering student feedback on financial aid policies and identifying student needs
- **Knowledge outreach**: Promoting credit, financial, and aid policy literacy through class meetings, talks, and quiz competitions to raise awareness of illegal lending and telecom fraud
- **Contest participation**: Encouraging students to enter the 6th "Guard Your Wallet" anti-illegal finance short video contest
- **Custom activities**: Each college is encouraged to design its own integrity education events based on its unique strengths

## Requirements

The Student Affairs Office has asked all colleges to mobilize broad student participation, integrate integrity education into campus culture, and publicize their activities through campus and external media. Activity statistics must be submitted to the Student Financial Aid Management Center at scholarship@stu.edu.cn by June 3.
