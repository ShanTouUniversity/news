## Overview

On May 17, 2026, the STU Business School MBA Education Center hosted a competition experience sharing session at Sangpushan Campus. The event featured 2024 cohort student representatives who were members of the 2025 National Management Case Competition South China runner-up team.

The National Management Case Competition, guided by the National MBA Education Steering Committee, is a major case competition for MBA students focusing on management theory application, business problem analysis, teamwork, and presentation skills.

## Key Takeaways

Student representatives provided a comprehensive overview covering competition rules, team formation, preparation planning, and practical techniques. They walked through every key milestone from case release to blind review submission, live presentation, and defense:

- **Teamwork**: Rational division of labor based on individual strengths across case study, industry analysis, problem diagnosis, solution design, slide creation, and live presentation, refined through multiple rounds of discussion and mock presentations
- **Material design**: Differentiated approaches for blind review slides versus live presentation slides, balancing structure, analytical depth, business logic, and visual impact
- **Defense tactics**: Accurately interpreting questions, organizing responses quickly, highlighting key points, and maintaining composure during live sessions

## Interactive Q&A

Attendees actively asked about registration, team building, preparation pacing, case analysis methods, and handling on-stage surprises, with the speakers addressing each question based on their firsthand experience.
